The Nakba ("Catastrophe") (1948)

The Nakba, meaning "catastrophe" in Arabic, refers to the mass displacement and dispossession of Palestinians during the 1948 Arab-Israeli War. Over 750,000 Palestinians were forcibly expelled from their homes, and over 500 villages were destroyed. As a result, Israel was established on 78% of historic Palestine.
