First Intifada (1987-1993)

The First Intifada, also known as the "Stone Intifada," was a mass popular uprising by Palestinians against the Israeli occupation of the West Bank and Gaza Strip. It began in December 1987 and lasted until the Madrid Conference in 1991, although some consider it to have ended with the signing of the Oslo Accords in 1993. The uprising was characterized by widespread civil disobedience, strikes, boycotts, and stone-throwing by Palestinian youth against Israeli soldiers.
