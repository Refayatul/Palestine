Naksa (Six-Day War) (1967)

The Naksa, meaning "setback" or "defeat" in Arabic, refers to the Six-Day War in 1967, during which Israel occupied the remaining 22% of Palestine (West Bank, Gaza Strip, and East Jerusalem), as well as the Syrian Golan Heights and the Egyptian Sinai Peninsula. The war resulted in the displacement of another 300,000 Palestinians.
