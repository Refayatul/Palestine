UN Partition Plan (Resolution 181) (1947)

The UN Partition Plan, also known as Resolution 181, was a United Nations proposal in 1947 to divide the British Mandate of Palestine into separate Arab and Jewish states. The plan proposed giving 55% of Palestine to the Jewish state, despite Jews owning only 7% of the land and comprising only 33% of the population.
